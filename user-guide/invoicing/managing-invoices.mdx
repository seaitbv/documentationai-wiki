title: "Managing invoices"
description: "View, manage, and track effective invoices and credit notes in ARMS."
---

The effective invoices tab gives you a complete view of all invoices and credit notes created in ARMS. From here you can review invoice details, track payment statuses, and manage the invoice lifecycle.

## Invoice list columns

| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Invoice number** | Unique invoice identifier |
| **Customer** | Customer name |
| **Date** | Invoice creation date |
| **Due date** | Payment due date (based on payment conditions from the contract) |
| **Type** | Invoice, Advance invoice, Deposit invoice, or Credit note |
| **Total (excl. VAT)** | Subtotal before VAT |
| **Total VAT** | VAT amount |
| **Total (incl. VAT)** | Grand total including VAT |
| **Status (ARMS)** | Created, Sent (exported), Paid, or Cancelled |
| **Status (Exact Online)** | Payment status from Exact Online: Open, Partially paid, or Fully paid |

## Default filter

By default, the list shows invoices with status **Created** and **Sent** (exported). Paid and cancelled invoices are hidden but available by expanding the filter.

## Invoice status management

Invoices follow a defined status flow:

| From | To | Action |
|------|----|--------|
| Created | Sent | Export to Exact Online |
| Created | Cancelled | Cancel the invoice |
| Sent | Paid | Payment confirmed (from Exact Online) |
| Sent | Cancelled | Cancel the invoice |

<Callout kind="info">
  The "Paid" status is typically updated automatically based on payment information returned from Exact Online. You do not need to mark invoices as paid manually in most cases.
</Callout>

## Invoice detail view

Click any invoice row to open the full detail view. The detail screen contains:

### Header

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Supplier** | Name, address, and VAT number of Atrac or Urbain |
| **Customer** | Official name, address, and VAT number |
| **Invoice number** | The ARMS invoice number |
| **Invoice date** | Date the invoice was created |
| **Due date** | Payment deadline based on the contract's payment conditions |
| **Payment reference** | Structured communication (generated by Exact Online after export) |

### Invoice lines

Each invoice contains one or more lines:

| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **#** | Line number |
| **Description** | What is being billed (rental, insurance, deduction, advance offset) |
| **Quantity** | Number of units |
| **Unit** | Day, month, km, or unit |
| **Unit price** | Price per unit |
| **Discount** | Discount percentage (rental lines only) |
| **VAT %** | Applicable VAT rate |
| **Total excl. VAT** | Line total before VAT |

### Totals

- Subtotal (excl. VAT)
- VAT breakdown by rate (e.g., 21%: X EUR, 0%: Y EUR)
- **Total payable (incl. VAT)**

## Advance offset

When an advance payment was made at the start of the contract, ARMS automatically deducts the advance from the first regular invoice. This appears as a negative line on the invoice:

| Line | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Advance offset** | Negative amount deducted from the invoice total, 0% VAT |

The offset amount equals the lesser of the remaining advance balance and the invoice subtotal. ARMS tracks the cumulative offset so subsequent invoices are not double-deducted.

<Callout kind="info">
  The advance offset only appears on the **first** regular invoice after the advance was paid. If the advance exceeds the first invoice subtotal, the remaining balance carries over to the next invoice.
</Callout>

## Creating a manual invoice

In addition to generating invoices from proposals, you can create invoices manually:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Manual invoice" icon="file-plus" titleType="p">
    On the effective invoices tab, click the **Manual invoice** button.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select a contract" icon="link" titleType="p">
    Choose the contract to link the invoice to. Every invoice in ARMS must be linked to a contract.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add invoice lines" icon="edit" titleType="p">
    Manually enter the invoice lines with descriptions, quantities, unit prices, and VAT rates.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save the invoice" icon="save" titleType="p">
    Click **Save** to create the invoice. It follows the same validation and export flow as generated invoices.
  </Step>
</Steps>
